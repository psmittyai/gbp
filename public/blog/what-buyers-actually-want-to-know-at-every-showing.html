What Buyers Actually Want to Know at Every Showing

May 30, 2026 · 3 min read
```html

The Questions That Actually Close Showings

Buyers in 2026 aren't asking about square footage anymore—they're asking about *your* confidence. They want to know if you genuinely believe this property solves their problem, not just if it checks boxes. Most agents miss this shift entirely.

Real estate buyer questions have evolved. Today's buyers ask about energy costs, work-from-home viability, neighborhood safety data, and resale potential. They want specifics, not fluff.

What Every Buyer Needs Before They Decide

First: property taxes and actual monthly carrying costs. Buyers hate surprises. A quick property tax breakdown before they fall in love with a home saves everyone heartbreak later.

Second: the neighborhood story. Crime rates, school ratings, commute times, upcoming development. Buyers want context, not just location.

Third: honest answers about what's wrong with the property. The roof's 15 years old? Say it upfront. Buyers respect transparency more than you think.

Your Competitive Edge Isn't Luck

Agents who answer these questions *at* the showing—not after—win the listing. You need live property reports, tax estimates, and market comps ready to pull up on your phone. No fumbling. No "I'll send that over."

That's not extra work—that's just being prepared. And when you're prepared, buyers feel it. They trust you because you've done the homework they couldn't do themselves. When you're ready with the right intel, you close the showing before the buyer even realizes they want to make an offer.
```
