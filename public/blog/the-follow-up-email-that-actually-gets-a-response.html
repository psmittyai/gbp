The Follow-Up Email That Actually Gets a Response

May 31, 2026 · 3 min read

Why Your Follow-Ups Disappear Into the Void

You send a thoughtful email. Radio silence. Then another. Still nothing. The problem isn't your persistence—it's that your follow-up sounds like every other agent's follow-up. Generic. Forgettable. Dead on arrival.

Leads don't ignore you because they're not interested. They ignore you because your message doesn't feel personal or valuable enough to respond to.

The Real Secret to Getting Replies

Stop selling. Start adding value. A follow-up that includes something useful—a recent market update for their neighborhood, a specific property insight, or a genuine question about their timeline—feels less like noise and more like help.

The best follow-ups reference something from your last conversation, show you've done your homework, and make it easy for them to say yes. Three sentences. One clear ask. Done.

The Follow-Up That Works

Here's what separates responses from silence: specificity plus urgency plus respect for their time. Your follow-up should feel like it was written for them alone—because it was.
