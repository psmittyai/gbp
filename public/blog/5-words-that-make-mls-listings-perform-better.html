5 Words That Make MLS Listings Perform Better

May 30, 2026 · 3 min read

The Words That Actually Move Listings

Here's the thing: most MLS listings sound the same. "Charming," "updated," "great bones"—agents have been using these words since 2015. In 2026, buyers are tired of generic descriptions. They want specificity. They want to feel something.

The five words that consistently outperform? Light, flow, view, timeline, and story. Light sells emotion. Flow sells lifestyle. View sells aspiration. Timeline sells urgency (without being pushy). Story sells the reason someone should actually care.

Why These Words Win

Light doesn't just describe a room—it tells buyers they'll wake up happy there. Flow means they can picture their Saturday morning coffee moving through the space. A view isn't just scenery; it's the reason they picked this neighborhood in the first place.

Timeline matters because buyers want to know: is this move urgent or flexible? Story is the secret weapon. A one-sentence note about the original architect, the heritage trees, or the neighborhood's evolution turns a property into a reason to buy.
