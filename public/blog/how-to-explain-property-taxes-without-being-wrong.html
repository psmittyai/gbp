How to Explain Property Taxes Without Being Wrong

May 31, 2026 · 3 min read

The Property Tax Talk Nobody Wants to Have

Your buyers ask about property taxes. You freeze a little. Is it the assessed value? The millage rate? How much do you actually need to know?

Here's the truth: you don't need to *be* a tax accountant. You just need to explain it clearly enough that buyers stop feeling blindsided at closing.

Break It Down Into Three Parts

Property taxes = assessed home value × tax rate. That's it. The assessed value is what the county says your home is worth (often lower than market value). The tax rate is set by your local government and varies wildly by county.

When a buyer says "how much will I pay?", you can actually *calculate* that number instead of guessing. Use your property tax calculator to pull real numbers in seconds—no spreadsheets, no second-guessing yourself.

Why Buyers Care (and Why You Should Lead With This)

Taxes aren't abstract. They're $200-500 added to every mortgage payment in most markets. That changes affordability. A buyer approved for $400K might only comfortably buy a $350K home once taxes hit their budget.

Bring it up early—during qualification, not at the last second. Smart agents explain taxes the same way they explain rate locks and inspection contingencies: as a normal part of the deal. No drama, no surprises.

One Tool, One Tap, Real Answers

When you're running qualifying conversations with leads, you can pull actual tax data in real time. Plug in the address, see the number, move forward. Your buyers trust you because you have answers—not because you're pretending.

That's how you stop being the agent who shrugs at tax season and start being the one who explains property taxes like you actually know what you're talking about.
